# CIP vs Salesforce (F&B) — discovery questions + leave-behind

_GTM collateral for the deal where **KPMG has recommended Salesforce** to an F&B group (recommendation
only — nothing bought). Goal: diagnose the real job, then position CIP as the **necessary** F&B layer
(Salesforce = optional). Don't attack KPMG; reframe the question. Cost figures are public benchmarks —
verify against the actual quote. See also `docs/business/positioning.md`, memory `moat-register`._

## PART 1 — Discovery questions (diagnose before you pitch)

Ask these of the client (and, where you can, KPMG). Each has a **"what the answer tells you"** so you can
steer to *replace* (CIP is the CRM) vs *coexist* (CIP feeds Salesforce) vs *walk* (genuine enterprise fit).

### A. The real job — what should "CRM" actually do?
1. When you say "we need a CRM," what's the **outcome** you're buying — more repeat visits, win-back, knowing your diners, running promos, or head-office reporting? → *Retention/diner outcomes = CIP's job. "Single enterprise platform" = possible Salesforce fit.*
2. Walk me through what you'd want it to do **on a normal trading day**, at the outlet and at HQ. → *Surfaces daily-driver 20% vs checkbox features nobody uses.*
3. Who are the daily **users** — outlet managers/cashiers, or a head-office marketing team? → *Frontline = CIP; large HQ org with many departments = breadth need.*

### B. The capture reality (exposes Salesforce's blind spot — ask gently)
4. Today, **how do you know who your customers are** — do you capture identity at the point of the meal? → *Usually "we don't / loosely." This is the gap Salesforce can't fill.*
5. What % of your sales are tied to a **known customer** right now? → *Low % = the priority is capture (CIP), not a CRM to file data you don't have.*
6. How will customer data **get into** whatever system you pick — who captures it, where? → *Salesforce is an empty shell; this question makes the capture problem visible.*

### C. Scope & breadth (replace vs coexist)
7. Is this for the **F&B business only**, or group-wide across other business lines? → *F&B-only → CIP replaces. Conglomerate/multi-vertical → coexist.*
8. Do you need **B2B / corporate catering / account management**, or is it B2C diners? → *B2B need = Salesforce has a real role; B2C-only = CIP.*
9. Do you need enterprise **governance** (approvals, service/case management, group-wide security)? → *Yes = coexist; no = CIP alone.*

### D. Why Salesforce / KPMG's rationale (the pivotal one)
10. What did KPMG base the Salesforce recommendation on — your stated needs, or a standard enterprise default? → *Distinguishes genuine-fit vs lazy-default.*
11. Did the evaluation include **F&B-specific** options (capture, loyalty, ordering), or only horizontal CRMs? → *Often only horizontal — opens the door for a fair F&B comparison.*
12. Is KPMG also **implementing** it? Over what timeline and fee? → *Surfaces the SI-fee dynamic + the real cost/time.*

### E. Cost, time, risk, success
13. What's the **all-in budget** — licences **plus** implementation, plus internal effort? → *Anchors the six-figure reality vs CIP's cost.*
14. When do you need to see **measurable value** (e.g. repeat-visit lift)? → *If "soon," a 6-month build is a problem; pitch the CIP pilot.*
15. How will you **measure success** in 6–12 months? → *If it's diner metrics (2nd-visit, win-back, repeat rate), that's CIP's home turf.*
16. What's your appetite for **implementation risk** (Salesforce projects commonly overrun)? → *Sets up the low-risk pilot.*

### F. Decision process
17. Who **owns the final decision** — and is it signed, or still open? → *Confirms the window is open.*
18. What would make you confident enough to **pilot an F&B-purpose-built option** alongside the evaluation? → *Gets a foot in with a low-commitment yes.*

> **Read-out:** mostly A/B/E answers about *diners, capture, fast value, tight budget* → **lead CIP (replace)**.
> Strong C answers about *group-wide, B2B, governance* → **lead coexist (CIP feeds Salesforce)**.

## PART 2 — Leave-behind (one page for the room)

### The question isn't "which CRM." It's "how do we capture and grow our diners?"

A horizontal CRM is a filing cabinet. It can't capture a diner at the table, run your loyalty, or take an
order. For an F&B group, that capability is **necessary** — and it's exactly what CIP does.

**So the real choice is layered, not either/or:**

| | **NECESSARY — the F&B engine** | **OPTIONAL — the enterprise platform** |
|---|---|---|
| | **CIP** | **Salesforce** |
| Capture diner identity at the meal (QR-at-table) | ✅ day one | ❌ can't |
| Menu / ordering / POS | ✅ | ❌ |
| Loyalty (coins, vouchers, games) + SG coalition | ✅ | partial (paid add-on) |
| RFM segmentation + AI next-best-action | ✅ | add-on (Einstein) |
| Win-back & campaigns (WhatsApp) | ✅ | add-on (Marketing Cloud) |
| Enterprise B2B / accounts / service / governance | — (not its job) | ✅ |
| Group-wide system of record, 1000s of integrations | — | ✅ |

**You need the left column regardless.** The only question is whether you *also* need the right column.

### Cost & time to value (public benchmarks — verify vs your quote)
| | CIP | Salesforce (for F&B) |
|---|---|---|
| Licence | per-outlet subscription, ~0 commission | ~US$165/user/mo (Enterprise) + Marketing/Data/Loyalty add-ons |
| Implementation | days; self-serve onboarding | SI project (often **six figures**, **months**) |
| Time to first value | diners captured **week 1** | after the build ships |
| Built for F&B | yes — purpose-built | no — generic, needs heavy customisation |

### The KPI math: $26M → +$2.6M — where the growth comes from
The client's KPI is **+10% transactions = +SGD 2.6M/year.** A growth number is bought with a growth engine,
not a system of record. (Ticket/volume illustrative — plug in the client's real figures; the shape holds.)

- $26M ÷ ~$25 avg ticket ≈ **~1.04M transactions/year** → +10% = **+104k transactions/yr ≈ ~285/day** across the group.
- **+10% is one more visit per regular diner, per year** — exactly what RFM-targeted offers, loyalty + win-back deliver.
- Or: lift **2nd-visit conversion** (industry ~15% → ~40% with a welcome/return offer) — each convert adds a diner's *lifetime* visits.
- **The $26M of data is the fuel** — it powers CIP's RFM + AI to target the right diner with the right offer from day one (your data, your KPI).
- **Even a 1% lift = $260k** — pays for CIP many times over. **Salesforce contributes ~$0 directly to this KPI** (it stores; it doesn't grow).

> **Salesforce stores your $26M. CIP grows it by $2.6M.**

### Two honest ways forward
- **CIP-led (recommended if the job is diner growth):** CIP is your customer engine. Add Salesforce later only if enterprise breadth demands it.
- **Coexist (if you have genuine group-wide/B2B needs):** keep Salesforce as the system of record; **CIP captures the diner and feeds Salesforce the cleanest data it will ever have.** CIP makes a Salesforce rollout *succeed* — implementations fail on missing source data, which is precisely what CIP supplies.

We're not anti-Salesforce. We're the layer it can't replace.

### De-risk it: a 4–6 week pilot
One brand or outlet. We prove the **2nd-visit lift, win-back, and repeat-rate** on your real diners — live,
in weeks, at minimal cost — before any platform-scale commitment. A working result beats a roadmap.

**Bottom line:** CIP is the *necessary* engine that captures and grows your diners. Salesforce is an
*optional* enterprise platform on top. Start with what's necessary; prove it in weeks.

## PART 3 — The pitch (talk-track for the room)

**The whole battle is won by changing the question.** KPMG framed it as *"which CRM?"* (you lose that —
enterprise checkboxes). You reframe it as *"how do we hit +10%?"* (you win that — it's what you're built
for). Whoever frames the question wins.

### 30-second hook (open with this)
> *"Your KPI this year is to grow transactions 10% — that's +$2.6 million. So the real question isn't
> 'which CRM platform' — it's 'what actually produces that $2.6M?' Salesforce is a brilliant system of
> record. But it doesn't grow revenue — it stores it. CIP is a growth engine built for one job: turn your
> diners into repeat diners. Let me show you the math, then let's prove it on a pilot before anyone signs."*

### The 5 beats
1. **Anchor on their number.** "Measure everything today against one thing: **+$2.6M.** If it doesn't move that number, it's overhead."
2. **Necessary vs optional.** "CIP is the *necessary* F&B engine — captures the diner, runs loyalty, drives repeat visits. Salesforce is the *optional* enterprise platform. You need CIP regardless of what HQ runs."
3. **You can't grow what you can't see.** "Your $26M is anonymous today. Salesforce arrives empty — it can't capture a diner at the table. CIP captures the identity that makes +10% possible. Step one of your KPI is the one thing Salesforce structurally cannot do."
4. **The math.** "$26M, ~$25 a ticket ≈ a million transactions. **+10% is one more visit per regular, per year** — exactly what RFM offers + win-back do. Your $26M is the fuel for our AI. Even a **1% lift is $260k**."
5. **De-risk → easy yes.** "One brand, 4–6 weeks, *measure the actual lift.* A real number beats a roadmap. Works → scale; doesn't → you risked almost nothing."

### 4 lines to memorise
1. *"Salesforce stores your $26M. CIP grows it by $2.6M."*
2. *"You can't grow what you can't see — and Salesforce can't see the diner at the table."*
3. *"+10% is one more visit per regular, per year. That's our home turf."*
4. *"Buy the growth engine, not the filing cabinet."*

### Objection one-liners
- **"KPMG recommended Salesforce."** → *"And they're right — for enterprise governance. Your KPI is growth, that's a different tool. Happy to feed Salesforce later; today let's hit the number."* (Never attack KPMG — give them a future role.)
- **"We want one platform."** → *"You'll have one customer record — CIP captures it and can pipe it straight into Salesforce. We make a Salesforce rollout *succeed*; those projects fail on missing data, which is exactly what we supply."*
- **"Is CIP enterprise-grade?"** → *"Purpose-built where Salesforce is generic. For the enterprise checkboxes — SSO, audit, export — we have the essentials, and you keep Salesforce for the rest."*
- **"Salesforce has loyalty too."** → *"A paid add-on that still needs a capture + POS layer it doesn't have. We're the engine *and* the capture — plus the SG coalition."*

### The ask (close)
> *"Let me run a 4–6 week pilot on one brand, measured against your +10% KPI. Give me the diners; I'll show
> you the lift. Then you decide — with data, not a slide."*

**Why this wins:** you stop competing on CRM features (Salesforce's turf, where you lose) and compete on
*who delivers the +$2.6M* (your turf, where Salesforce can't even start — it can't capture a diner). You're
not anti-Salesforce, so KPMG can't call you reckless — you just made yourself *necessary* and Salesforce *optional*.
